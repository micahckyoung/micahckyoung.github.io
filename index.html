Micah Young My Work

Hi, I’m Micah. I grew up in the San Francisco Bay Area and went to college at Yale. Following an interest in social science and history, I double majored in Economics and African Studies. I believe that Data Science can be used to improve the lives of indigenous peoples: from mainland Native Americans to Africans, or Native Hawaiians such as myself. While data are informative, they are incomplete without cultural understanding. Meaningful research must come from within the community, rather than technically proficient outsiders. My goal is to develop skills in order to uplift and empower my community of Native Hawaiians.

In my free time, I read books. Every six months or so I dive into a new subject area. I’ve studied poetry and Russian literature, to critical race theory and African literature, to memoirs, biographies, philosophy, and history of science. I keep track of my readings and write annual reflections of what I’ve learned.

Post-college, I was an Associate Consultant at Bain & Company and I currently work as a Data Scientist for Berkshire Partners.
